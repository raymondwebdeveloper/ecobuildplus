New Construction FAQ

Is It Cheaper to Build a New House or Buy an Existing One?

Whether it is cheaper to build a new house or buy an existing one depends on your budget and timeline in the Chicago area. In most cases, buying is cheaper upfront, while building can deliver better long-term value through energy efficiency, layout fit, lower maintenance, and modern amenities.

What Is the Most Expensive Part of New Home Construction?

The most expensive part of new home construction is typically site work, foundation, framing, and core systems like plumbing, electrical, HVAC, and insulation. However, high-spec exterior envelope items (such as premium windows and roofing) and custom interior elements like cabinetry, tile, and built-in features can rival those costs depending on the level of customization and materials selected.

What Is the Best Time of Year to Build a House?

The best time of year to build a house is often spring or early fall in Chicagoland, when deep freezes and heavy rains are less likely. Interior phases can still move forward in other seasons with proper planning, weather protection, reliable scheduling, and an experienced builder overseeing the work.

What Is the Hardest Stage in Building a House?

The hardest stage in building a house is coordinating budget, selections, permits, inspections, and trades on schedule. Early phases like site work and foundation set the tone, and clear communication with an experienced general contractor keeps the process on track.

How Is a Luxury Home Built Faster Without Sacrificing Custom Quality?

A luxury home is built faster without sacrificing custom quality by utilizing the Signature Series, which features a curated collection of high-end floor plans that bypass the typical 12-month architectural design phase. These plans are specifically engineered for Northern Illinois lots, offering a predictable path to a new home while still allowing for total customization of interior requests and material sourcing.

What Is the Typical Timeline From the Initial Consultation to Move-in Day?

The typical timeline from the initial consultation to move-in day is streamlined through a proven 8-step design-build process. This journey begins with an initial consultation and lot assessment, moving through design and construction, and concludes with final inspections and the official closing where keys are handed over.

How Is a New Home Designed to Ensure Lower Monthly Operating Costs?

A new home is designed to ensure lower monthly operating costs through Future-Proof Engineering, which incorporates superior insulation and high-efficiency mechanical systems. Additionally, we utilize Shadow-Free Siting to orient the home on your lot for maximum natural light and thermal efficiency, alongside airtight construction to keep you comfortable all the time while reducing energy bills.

Are There Any Hidden Fees or Costs Typically Found in the Custom Home Building Process?

A Northbrook custom home builder provides comprehensive services from start to finish, including site selection, design collaboration, permitting, and full project management. They handle construction, coordinate subcontractors, and complete all interior and exterior finishes. Builders also offer smart home technology integration and energy-efficient solutions. After completion, they provide post-construction support and warranties, ensuring your home meets the highest standards and your experience is smooth from concept to move-in.

What Is the Process for the “Final Inspection” Before I Am Handed the Keys?

The process for the “final inspection” before you are handed the keys involves a meticulous white-glove walkthrough and the coordination of all required municipal building inspections. This stage confirms every interior detail, down to the smallest element, is exactly as designed and that the home meets the highest standards of structural integrity before the final closing.
