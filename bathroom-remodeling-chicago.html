Frequently Asked Questions

What Is the Most Expensive Part of a Bathroom Remodel?

The most expensive part of a bathroom remodel is typically skilled labor for plumbing and waterproofing, since altering pipes and protecting wet areas requires licensed professionals and precise installation. High-end fixtures and custom tile work can also significantly increase overall project costs when premium materials and detailed designs are selected.

Do I Need a Permit to Remodel My Bathroom in Chicago?

You typically need a permit to remodel your bathroom in Chicago if the project involves plumbing, electrical work, mechanical systems, structural changes, or layout modifications. Minor cosmetic updates such as painting or basic tile replacement may not require a permit, but it is always best to confirm requirements with professionals before beginning work.

How Is Plumbing Handled When Relocating a Sink or Toilet?

Plumbing is handled by rerouting existing supply, drainage, waste, and vent lines through walls or floors while maintaining proper slope and code compliance. New rough-in components are installed before walls are closed to ensure reliable performance and prevent future leaks.

How Is a Remodeling Timeline Managed so I Can Get My Bathroom Back Faster?

A remodeling timeline is managed through a detailed pre-construction phase that finalizes design selections and material orders before demolition begins. By coordinating trades in advance and securing long-lead materials early, the on-site construction phase moves efficiently and stays within a streamlined schedule.

Will I Be Able to Use My Other Home Bathrooms During Construction?

Yes, you can continue using other bathrooms in your home during construction. The work zone is carefully isolated and protected daily to maintain cleanliness and keep the rest of your home functional while the remodel is underway.

How Can a Small Bathroom Be Redesigned to Feel More Spacious?

A small bathroom can feel more spacious by replacing bulky tubs with sleek, glass-enclosed walk-in showers and installing floating vanities to open up floor space. Large-format tiles with minimal grout lines and well-planned lighting further enhance the sense of openness and reduce visual clutter.

What Type of Waterproofing Is Used Behind New Shower and Floor Tiles?

Advanced waterproofing systems such as liquid-applied membranes or preformed sheet membranes are installed behind shower and floor tiles to create a continuous moisture barrier. These systems protect structural materials from water intrusion and help prevent mold and long-term damage.
