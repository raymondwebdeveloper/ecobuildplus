Remodeling Experts of Chicago FAQ

Do I Need a Building Permit for Remodeling in the Chicago Area?

You do need a building permit for remodeling in the Chicago area for most projects that go beyond basic cosmetic updates. In general, if your remodel involves structural changes, new or relocated plumbing, new or modified electrical work, or mechanical system changes, permits are required through the City of Chicago or your local municipality. Consulting EcoBuild Plus or your local building department is essential to confirm what your specific project requires.

Is It More Cost-Effective to Remodel a Home or Build One?

It is more cost-effective to remodel a home than to build one in many Chicago-area cases, especially when the existing structure is sound and you focus on targeted updates rather than a full gut renovation. In situations where the home has major structural issues, extensive code problems, or needs a completely new layout and systems, building new can sometimes be the better long-term value. The best way to decide is to have EcoBuild Plus review your home and provide a project-specific cost comparison for remodeling versus building new.

What Should I Remodel First?

You should remodel the most heavily used, high-impact spaces first, typically the kitchen and bathrooms. Once those are addressed, the next priority is fixing any structural or safety issues such as roofing, foundation problems, or outdated electrical and plumbing. After that, you can move on to secondary spaces and aesthetic upgrades in line with your budget and goals.

When Should You Remodel Your Home?

When you should remodel your home depends on your personal needs and situation, including necessary repairs, long-term plans for selling, your budget, and your lifestyle goals. EcoBuild Plus can help you weigh these factors and determine the best timing for your project based on your specific circumstances.

Which Room Is the Most Popular to Remodel?

The kitchen is the most popular room to remodel, since updating this high-use space has a major impact on both daily functionality and home value. Bathrooms are usually next in line, because adding or upgrading a bath can significantly improve comfort for current owners and appeal for future buyers.

How Are New High-Efficiency Systems Integrated Into an Older Home?

New high-efficiency systems are integrated into an older home through Future-Proof Engineering, where the property is planned as one unified system to incorporate better insulation and smart infrastructure. This approach allows modern mechanical units to work seamlessly within an existing footprint, leading to lower operating costs and easier future upgrades.

What Happens if Outdated Electrical or Plumbing Is Found Behind the Walls?

If outdated electrical or plumbing is found behind the walls, seasoned professionals utilize Expert Oversight to manage the situation and meet current safety standards. Because we specialize in complex structural transformations, we execute the foundational details carefully to protect your investment and ensure long-term performance.

Are Specialized Tests Required for Lead or Asbestos in Older Chicagoland Properties?

Specialized tests are often required for lead or asbestos in older Chicagoland properties to ensure a healthy living environment. As your remodeling partner, we manage these complexities during demolition and structural phases, following proper safety protocols to make every transformation safe and compliant.

What Is the Process for Choosing Energy-Efficient Upgrades That Actually Lower Bills?

The process for choosing energy-efficient upgrades that actually lower bills involves an Integrated Design Flow, where product costs and benefits are analyzed to help you select sustainable options. We focus on combining energy-efficient upgrades and high-efficiency systems that work together to maximize long-term savings and comfort.

What Is the Process for Approving Changes Once the Construction Has Already Started?

The process for approving changes once construction has started is managed through a structured milestone system that keeps customization and special requests organized. Even after construction begins, transparent communication ensures any design updates or material sourcing changes reflect your vision without compromising the overall timeline.
