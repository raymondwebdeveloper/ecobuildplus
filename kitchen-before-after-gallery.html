LUXURY MODERN FARMHOUSE
KITCHEN TRANSFORMATION

See how we transformed a dark, dated layout into a stunning Luxury Modern Farmhouse Kitchen. Featuring custom white shaker cabinets, premium quartz countertops, and professional-grade finishes, this bright and airy renovation proves how smart design can completely redefine the heart of your home.

The Transformation:

  • Cabinetry: Replaced dark, soffit-limited oak with Custom White Shaker Cabinetry. Uppers now extend to the ceiling to maximize storage; added glass-front display uppers and brushed nickel hardware.
  • Countertops: Swapped laminate for premium White Quartz. Non-porous, heat-resistant, and maintenance-free.
  • Backsplash: Covered the dated red brick with Distressed Wood-Look Porcelain Tile. This achieves a rustic farmhouse texture with the durability/cleanability of tile.
  • Appliances: Upgraded to a professional KitchenAid Stainless Steel Suite, including a slide-in gas range and a high-CFM Stainless Chimney Hood for superior ventilation.
  • Flooring: Replaced patterned vinyl with Espresso Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP) or Engineered Hardwood for high contrast.
  • Lighting: Removed the bulky brass fan; installed a layered plan with recessed can lighting, under-cabinet task lighting, and a low-profile modern fan.

Kitchen Remodeling FAQ for Homeowners in the Chicago Area

What Is the Average Cost of a Kitchen Remodel in Chicagoland?

The average cost of a kitchen remodel in Chicagoland can vary significantly depending on the scope of work, the size of the kitchen, the materials selected, and the level of customization involved. In many parts of the Chicago area, mid-range kitchen remodels commonly fall in roughly the $35,000 to $75,000 range, while larger or high-end projects with custom cabinetry and premium appliances can run from about $80,000 to $150,000 or more, so getting a tailored estimate from EcoBuild Plus is the best way to understand your exact budget.

What’s the Most Expensive Part of a Chicago Kitchen Remodel?

The most expensive part of a Chicago kitchen remodel is typically the cabinetry, which often runs from about $10,000 to $30,000 or more depending on the size of the kitchen and whether you choose stock, semi-custom, or fully custom cabinets. Cabinets frequently account for a large share of the overall budget, and when combined with items like premium countertops or appliance packages, they can easily become the single biggest investment in the project.

What Does It Cost to Design a Kitchen?

It costs between about $1,500 and $7,500 or more to design a kitchen, depending largely on the complexity of the design and the size of the space. The cost structure can vary, with some designers charging an hourly rate and others using a flat fee or a percentage of the overall project cost, so it is wise to compare a few detailed quotes for your specific project.

Do I Need a Permit to Remodel My Kitchen in the Chicago Area?

You do need a permit to remodel your kitchen in the Chicago area for most projects that go beyond simple cosmetic updates. For example, if your remodel includes changing the layout, moving or adding plumbing or electrical, or doing any structural work to walls or openings, permits through the Chicago Department of Buildings or your local municipality are typically required, while like-for-like cabinet, countertop, or paint updates usually do not need one.

Why should I extend my upper cabinets all the way to the ceiling?

You should extend your upper cabinets all the way to the ceiling to maximize vertical storage for items like holiday platters or large pots. This design choice eliminates the “dust trap” gap found above standard units and creates a streamlined look that makes the room feel taller.

What is ‘layered lighting,’ and why is it better than just having a ceiling fan?

Layered lighting is a strategy that combines task and accent sources to create a multifaceted environment that a single ceiling fan cannot provide. By using under-cabinet lights for prep and recessed cans for overall brightness, you eliminate dark shadows to make the kitchen safer.

What specific upgrades make a kitchen truly “sustainable” in Chicago?

Specific upgrades that make a kitchen truly sustainable in Chicago include installing induction cooktops and energy-efficient lighting to reduce power consumption. Using eco-conscious building practices and durable materials like quartz or porcelain also helps your project deliver lasting value.

Are energy-efficient appliances worth the extra cost for a Chicago family?

Construction schedules are managed during extreme Chicago winter weather by prioritizing interior projects like kitchen or bathroom remodeling during the coldest months to keep your project moving regardless of outdoor conditions. For structural additions, we use tented heating and frost prevention to protect materials like concrete and masonry from freezing.

What are the best eco-friendly options for kitchen flooring and lighting?

The best eco-friendly options for kitchen flooring and lighting include durable Luxury Vinyl Plank and high-efficiency LED fixtures. These materials provide a low-maintenance solution that stands up to daily life while reducing the environmental footprint of your home.

Which kitchen upgrades offer the highest return on investment (ROI) in Chicagoland?

Kitchen upgrades that offer the highest return on investment in Chicagoland typically include modernizing cabinetry and updating major appliances. These high-impact changes significantly increase property value and help homeowners enjoy a more functional cooking environment.
